Lead Story - Week Opener

The Court Strikes. Trump Counters. Markets Ask: What Now?

The Supreme Court strikes down Trump's IEEPA tariffs in a 6-3 decision - and the president immediately pulls the next card from the deck. A historic rupture in trade policy that doesn't end the uncertainty, it transforms it.

Last Friday, the Supreme Court delivered a stinging blow to the Trump administration's tariff regime: the International Emergency Economic Powers Act does not authorize the president to unilaterally impose tariffs. An estimated $133.5 billion was collected in 2025 under this authority. The ruling arrived the way court decisions always do when they redistribute political arithmetic - with immediate market reaction and even more immediate political response.

Trump moved the same day: a new executive order imposing a flat 10% global tariff under Section 122 of the Trade Act of 1974. Brutally simple, legally more robust, and - critically - additive to existing sector tariffs still in place. Amazon jumped 2%, Home Depot gained ground. The market's initial reaction was bullish. Don't read too much into that.

02 The Macro Picture Global

USA Q4 GDP printed at +1.4% - far below the 2.5% consensus and the weakest quarterly reading since the first tariff shock in spring 2025. Core PCE inflation held at 3.0% in December. The combination reads classically stagflationary in tone, even if the Fed avoids the word. Rate cut pricing collapsed after the warm PCE print: March now at 5% probability (from 10%), April at 18% (from 30%), June at 57% (from 85%). Kevin Warsh as the nominated Fed Chair - his remarks during confirmation hearings will be the dominant market driver of the coming weeks.

Structural Trade - High Conviction

US goods deficit 2025: a record $1.24 trillion. Eurozone current account surplus in December: 14.6 billion euros - well above the 9.8 billion euro consensus. The structural divergence case for long EUR/USD is not speculation, it is accounting. ECB target: inflation at 2% by end of 2026. Euro strength is the logical consequence.

Japan CPI fell below 2% - optically dovish, but misleading. Core-core inflation sits at 2.6%, and the BoJ upgraded its FY2026 inflation forecast. An April hike remains the base case. The yen weakened on the CPI data - that is an entry point for JPY longs positioning for rate differential convergence as the Fed holds and the BoJ normalizes.

Australia The RBA hiked to 3.85%. Unemployment held at 4.1% (consensus 4.2%). Full-time jobs +50,500, net gain only 17,800 after part-time losses. PMI Composite cooled to 52.0 from 55.7. Solid, but decelerating.

South Korea Semiconductor exports doubled year-over-year - this is not just a Korean data point, it is the global AI capex barometer. The AI capex supercycle thesis gets hard-data support here. Alphabet: $185B capex plan for 2026. Meta, Microsoft, Amazon all expanding in lockstep. Markets refuse to celebrate unanimously - rightfully so, as return on capital remains the open question.

Open Range Research Perspective

The Supreme Court effect is being overestimated, the Section 122 effect underestimated. IEEPA tariffs were legally fragile - and the market knew it. The new 10% flat structure is legally sturdier, easier to enforce, and - for companies with global supply chains - harder to hedge. Amazon up 2% on Friday is a relief rally, not an all-clear. The real question for the coming quarters is not whether tariffs, but which tariff structure ultimately survives.

05 Three Theses - One Week Research

1. The Dollar Smile Breaks Down. The US trade deficit is structural, not cyclical. The Eurozone surprises on the current account. The Fed pauses longer than the market expected. The long EUR/USD trade has fundamental grounding, not just sentiment. Warsh's rhetoric this week could be the catalyst.

2. AI Capex Return - The Moment of Truth Approaches. Alphabet, Amazon, Meta are guiding $180-185B in capex for 2026. Markets accepted this uncritically for a long time. Q1 earnings season starting in April will produce the first real data on cloud revenue growth that needs to justify this spending. The spread between capex growth and revenue growth is risk indicator number one for the tech sector.

3. Bitcoin Between $60K Floor and $85K Resistance. The 200-week moving average and the long-term holder zone at $60-65K remain the next gravitational center if equity markets rotate. Prediction markets assign a 29% probability to a new ATH in 2026. The Ethereum/Bitcoin ratio is approaching a historically bullish setup - structurally interesting, but not a trade for today.
