Analysis

Nvidia Delivered. The Market Didn't Care Enough. The Split Is Now Official.

Nvidia beat on every number: $68.13B revenue, $1.62 EPS, and a Q1 guide of $78B that towered above the $72.8B consensus. The stock rose 4% after hours. And yet US futures are modestly red this morning. Salesforce fell 5% on in-line guidance. Snowflake fell 2%. The market has made its decision: AI infrastructure is confirmed, the application layer is not.

Open Range Capital Research · Marcus Drauschke · Feb 26, 2026

The verdict on the AI trade was supposed to come last night. It did, and it was complicated. Nvidia reported Q4 revenue of $68.13 billion against a $66.21 billion consensus, with earnings per share of $1.62 against a $1.53 estimate. Its Q1 FY2027 revenue guidance of approximately $78 billion crushed a $72.8 billion Wall Street estimate by more than 7%. Jensen Huang noted that Vera Rubin samples have already shipped to customers this week, ahead of schedule. The data center business alone generated $62.3 billion, up 75% year over year, and now constitutes over 91% of total revenue. By every measure, the AI infrastructure super-cycle is not slowing.

And yet US equity futures are pointing lower this morning: S&P 6,938.5 (-0.11%), Nasdaq 100 25,277.0 (-0.21%), Dow 49,388.9 (-0.19%). This is not a collapse. But it is an answer. The market priced a near-perfect result into NVDA before the print, and even a blowout leaves little room for re-rating when expectations are this elevated. More importantly, Salesforce (-5% AH) and Snowflake (-2% AH) pulled the other direction. Both beat on revenue and earnings. Both gave guidance that was merely in line with estimates. In the current climate, "in line" reads as a soft miss. The SaaSpocalypse is not over. The market is cleaving the AI economy into two sub-economies: layer one, where Nvidia and the hyperscalers live, is confirmed and re-rated. Layer two, where Salesforce, Workday, and ServiceNow live, remains on trial.

The VIX tells an interesting intraday story. It closed Tuesday at 17.93 (-8.29%), a meaningful reduction in fear. But VIX futures are already pricing 19.59 (+0.72%) overnight. The cash VIX reflects yesterday's calm; the futures reflect tomorrow's uncertainty. Watch for whether the cash VIX re-converges upward at the open or the futures pull lower toward it. Bitcoin (+5.36%) and Ethereum (+9.75%) are the night's clearest risk-on signal, and that cuts both ways: it reflects genuine dollar-skepticism and liquidity appetite, but crypto is also the first asset class to absorb speculative positioning that has no obvious equity home.

Earnings Overnight · February 25, 2026 After Close
NVDA · Nvidia Beat · +4% AH
EPS: $1.62 vs $1.53 est Revenue: $68.13B vs $66.21B est (+73% YoY) Data Center: $62.3B (+75% YoY)

The Q1 FY2027 guide of approximately $78B against a $72.8B consensus was the number that mattered most, and it was unambiguous. Vera Rubin samples shipped to customers this week, ahead of schedule. Hyperscalers now account for just over 50% of data center revenue, confirming the capex cycle remains intact. The one soft note: gaming revenue fell 13% quarter over quarter as memory constraints force prioritization of AI accelerators. The broader verdict is that AI infrastructure spending is not slowing, and Nvidia remains the dominant pick-and-shovel play of the cycle. The 4% AH gain is modest given the beat magnitude, which is itself a signal: expectations were extremely elevated going in.

EPS: $3.81 vs $3.04 est Revenue: $11.20B vs $11.18B est (+12% YoY) FY27 Guide: ~$46B (in-line)

Operationally, this was a strong quarter: the fastest revenue growth in two years, a massive EPS beat, and 22,000+ Agentforce deals closed with Agentforce and Data Cloud ARR reaching $1.8B. Benioff announced a $50 billion share buyback, saying the stock is cheap at these prices after a 28% YTD decline. But the FY2027 guide of approximately $46B landed precisely in line with estimates, and in a market looking for evidence that Salesforce can accelerate above the AI disruption thesis, "in-line" is not enough. The market is asking whether agentic AI replaces Salesforce seats or expands them. Last night's print did not settle that question. The stock fell 5% despite beating on every backward-looking metric.

SNOW · Snowflake Beat · Guidance In-Line · -2% AH
Product Revenue: +30% YoY RPO: +42% Q1 FY27 Guide: ~$1.26B product revenue

A clean fundamental beat: 30% product revenue growth, 42% RPO expansion, and FY2027 guidance calling for 27% growth. Snowflake Intelligence adoption is described as one of the fastest product launches in company history. But the Q1 guide of approximately $1.26B was exactly in line with estimates, and with the stock already down 23% YTD and carrying the weight of the broader SaaS disruption thesis, the market needed more. Stock fell 2% AH. The grade: A- on fundamentals, C on narrative. Investors wanted a decisive refutation of the Workday/SaaSpocalypse concern, and "in line" guidance does not deliver that.

Investment Theses · Three Positions Worth Watching
Thesis I · AI Infrastructure vs. AI Application Layer
The Market Has Bifurcated. Pick Your Side.

Last night crystallized what has been building since the SaaSpocalypse on February 23: the AI economy now has two separate valuation regimes. Layer one, the infrastructure layer (NVDA, hyperscalers, custom silicon), has been validated by three consecutive quarters of accelerating capex and last night's $78B guide. This layer is pricing AI as a capital expenditure supercycle. Layer two, the application layer (CRM, WDAY, SNOW, ServiceNow), is pricing AI as an existential threat to per-seat revenue models. Salesforce's massive EPS beat and $50B buyback are Benioff's argument that the market has overstated the threat. The -5% AH move is the market's response: prove it with guidance, not buybacks.

The relevant reference for this newsletter's readers: the two-part SaaSpocalypse series published earlier this week (Part I: The Structural Revaluation in Application Software and Part II: Blue Owl and the Credit Market Dimension) argued that the disruption risk is structural and underpriced. Workday -10% AH last week, Salesforce -5% AH last night, and Snowflake -2% AH are consistent with that thesis. None of this has refuted it.

Thesis II · Precious Metals Complex
Gold at $5,211, Silver at $89: Consolidation, Not Reversal

Gold is down 0.29% overnight to $5,211 and silver is down 2.01% to $89.16. Neither move changes the structural picture. Gold remains within 2% of its January all-time high of $5,364. Silver is holding above $89 after touching $90+ this week. These are consolidation moves in the context of a commodity complex that has been pricing dollar debasement, tariff-driven inflation, and central bank Treasury rotation for three months. The macro thesis has not changed: EUR/USD is above 1.18, US yields are easing, and the 15% tariff regime is still pending formal executive order. The anti-dollar, anti-Treasury trade is intact. A move from $90 to $89 on silver is not a signal. It is noise.

Copper at $6.0320 (-0.19%) continues to hold near multi-year highs. WTI at $65.46 is stable. The commodity complex is not pricing a growth collapse; it is pricing repricing of dollar hegemony. That is a different and more durable thesis.

Thesis III · The 150-Day Tariff Clock
Day 4: Still No Executive Order. Still Not Good News.

Trump's 15% Section 122 tariff authority is still pending formal executive order as of Thursday morning. No new escalation emerged from the State of the Union Tuesday night, though Trump made the remarkable statement that he expects tariffs to eventually replace income taxes, which reads as ideological commitment rather than negotiating posture. The EU has held firm. The 150-day Congressional sunset window continues its countdown. Markets continue to read the absence of the formal order as a mild positive. It is not. It is suspended uncertainty with a fixed expiration date. 146 days remain.

Macro Watch · Japanese Government Bonds

The JGB Story Is Evolving

Yesterday's significant JGB spike (30Y +2.78%, 40Y +2.50%) is partially reversing overnight. The Japan 30Y is at 3.369% (-0.09%) and the 40Y is at 3.607% (-0.82%). But the Japan 10Y is still climbing: 2.153% (+1.03%). The picture is a short-end that continues to press higher against a long-end that is consolidating after yesterday's sharp move. This pattern is consistent with BoJ policy signaling rather than pure forced selling: the short end responds to rate expectations while the long end repriced on fiscal concerns and is now finding a level.

The structural point remains. As JGB yields rise, Japan's institutional investors (among the largest holders of US Treasuries and European sovereign debt) face less incentive to deploy capital abroad in search of yield. This is a slow-moving but important pressure on global capital flows. It does not resolve in a single session. Watch the Japan 10Y; if it continues through 2.2%, that is a new regime.
